## Notes

- No "temporary — replace before launch" competitor images were needed; every slot was filled with a
  genuine Wikimedia Commons file under a free license.
- The roofing-sheets category came up at 2 images rather than the requested 3-4. Several other Commons
  candidates for this topic were rejected during review: one showed heavy rust/decay (poor product
  representation), one had a competing brand name ("PRONTO") printed across roofing panels, and a set of
  1918 U.S. Army Signal Corps archival photos of the Youngstown Sheet and Tube Co. were rejected because
  the scanned archive card prints that company's name prominently across the image. Recommend sourcing
  one or two more roofing-sheet photos directly (e.g. a company site visit) before launch if a fuller
  set is wanted.
- `roofing-sheets-3.jpg` shows U.S. Navy Seabees insignia (a small unit patch on the helmets) — this is a
  military unit insignia, not a competing commercial brand, so it was kept in line with the "no other
  companies' logos" rule, but flagging here for visibility.
- All images were spot-checked at full resolution for readable text/logos before being kept. Several
  initially-downloaded candidates were discarded after this check: a steel-pipe factory railway-crossing
  photo naming a Chinese steel-pipe manufacturer, a pipe-yard photo with a crane operator's company
  branding, a steel-coil photo with visible construction-equipment brand names in the background, and
  three NARA/"Industries of War" archival scans that printed "Youngstown Sheet and Tube Co." across the
  frame.
